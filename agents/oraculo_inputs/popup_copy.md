# Pinnacle Holdings — Email Capture Popup Content

## Context
Popup appears 5 seconds after a visitor lands on pinnaclegroupwi.com (homepage, About, Services, Contact, News, FAQ — all public pages except /get-my-offer/). Bilingual: visitor sees EN or ES based on browser language. Dismissable; re-appears on next session until visitor subscribes.

## Business context
Pinnacle Holdings Group LLC is a Wisconsin-based cash home buyer (we-buy-houses operator). Audience: homeowners in distressed situations who need to sell quickly. Not an investor audience.

## Visual
- Dark green box (#0D3B2E) with gold accent (#C9A84C) and fuchsia border (#E83E8C)
- White modal on fuchsia-bordered green hero
- 72px modal, brand colors, mobile-first design

## Copy (English version presented here — Spanish mirror exists)

**Badge (small chip at top):** Wisconsin Homeowners

**Headline:** Thinking about selling? Know your options first.

**Subhead:** Free guides for homeowners facing foreclosure, inherited property, back taxes, or needing a fast sale — pros & cons of every path, plus monthly Wisconsin market updates. No pressure, unsubscribe anytime.

**Email input placeholder:** you@example.com

**CTA button:** Send Me Free Guides

**Decline link:** Maybe later

**Trust line:** Free · no obligation · Wisconsin-specific

## Success message (after email submit)

**Title:** You're in!

**Body:** Check your inbox — your first guide is on the way. You'll get monthly Wisconsin market updates and homeowner tips, no spam.

**Button:** Got it

## Goal

Grow a qualified list of distressed Wisconsin homeowners who are considering selling. The email list eventually gets nurtured with monthly market content + occasional direct offers ("We can make a cash offer within 24 hours"). High-intent subscribers will later fill out the full webform at /get-my-offer/.

## Key tradeoffs present in the copy

1. Lead magnet is "free guides" (plural, general) — not a specific named PDF. This gives us runway to send whatever first content we build, but might feel vague.
2. Promises "monthly market updates" — creates ongoing expectation; underdeliver hurts retention.
3. "Know your options first" positions Pinnacle as educational/neutral, not transactional. Builds trust but may slow the conversion to actual sellers.
4. "Pros & cons of every path" implies we'll cover options that DON'T benefit Pinnacle (e.g., "list on MLS"). Credibility-builder but potentially leaks audience to competitors.
