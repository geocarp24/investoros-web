# Target Audience — Wisconsin Distressed Homeowners

## Who they are

Homeowners in Wisconsin, predominantly age 45-70, who currently own a property they are considering selling — NOT because they are investors or upgrading, but because life circumstances are forcing the decision.

## Core segments

### Segment 1 — Pre-Foreclosure
- Missed 1-3 mortgage payments
- Received notice from lender or has lis pendens filed in Milwaukee / Waukesha / Dane / Brown counties
- Shame + panic mix, actively searching "stop foreclosure Wisconsin" or "sell house before foreclosure"
- Financial stress acute. Credit already damaged.
- Mostly lower-middle income ($45K–$75K household)

### Segment 2 — Inherited Property
- Parent or relative died, property now in probate or recently transferred
- Inheritor often lives out-of-state (common: adult child in IL, MN, or FL)
- Property likely has deferred maintenance; inheritor overwhelmed by logistics
- Wants off the obligation fast; MLS listing feels like too much work

### Segment 3 — Divorce / Separation
- Couple splitting, house is joint asset that must be liquidated
- One party wants fast sale to close chapter; other may resist
- Emotional + legal complexity, not just financial
- Often not ready for public listing ("don't want the neighborhood to know")

### Segment 4 — Behind on Taxes
- Property tax lien imminent or already attached
- Often happens with elderly owners on fixed income or vacant inherited properties
- Typically also has deferred maintenance
- Municipal sale deadline creates urgency

### Segment 5 — Tired Landlord / Bad Rental
- Owns a rental that's cash-flow negative or has problem tenants
- Property in poor condition, doesn't want to put more $ into it
- Wants to exit real estate entirely, not reinvest
- Middle-aged to senior, done with the hassle

### Segment 6 — Relocation / Job Loss
- Job transfer out of state, needs to sell within 30-60 days
- Or: laid off, can't afford the mortgage, needs to sell before arrears pile up
- Time pressure is the dominant factor
- Otherwise the home might sit well-maintained

## Behavioral traits common across segments

- **Mobile-first**: 60-70% of their research happens on their phone, not desktop
- **Skeptical of "we buy houses" cash buyers** — have heard they "lowball"
- **Value privacy** — don't want neighbors to know they're selling, don't want public MLS listing, don't want showings
- **Time-pressured** — typical target close window: 14-45 days
- **Avoid realtors** for this category — perceive 6% commission as unaffordable given their stress
- **Appreciate bilingual service** — Wisconsin has growing Spanish-speaking homeowner population, especially in Milwaukee, Green Bay, Racine

## Fears & objections

1. "They'll lowball me — I'll leave money on the table"
2. "This is too good to be true, must be a scam"
3. "I should just list it with a realtor, right?"
4. "I don't know what my house is worth"
5. "Will they actually close, or pull out last minute?"
6. "If I give them my email, they'll spam me forever"
7. "I don't have time to research 'cash buyer vs realtor' — just tell me the best option for MY situation"

## What they respond to

- **Empathy + no-pressure framing** ("know your options" resonates)
- **Education** (pros/cons of each path — they want the decision framework, not a pitch)
- **Social proof** (reviews, local case studies — but they rarely check detailed before committing)
- **Bilingual availability** (Spanish-speakers self-identify when available)
- **Privacy assurance** (no public listing, no signs in yard)
- **Speed commitments** ("cash offer in 24 hours" is a strong anchor)

## What they DON'T respond to

- Investor-speak ("deals", "off-market opportunities", "cap rate")
- Pushy CTAs ("Get Your Free Offer NOW")
- Generic national marketing that doesn't mention Wisconsin
- Long forms that demand too much info upfront
- Jargon (ARV, BRRRR, wholesale, etc.)

## Channels they use

- **Google search** (primary): "sell my house fast Milwaukee", "cash home buyers Wisconsin", "foreclosure help WI"
- **Facebook** (secondary): local community groups, especially Spanish-language
- **Direct mail** (they receive lots, mostly ignored; Pinnacle postcards perform better than competitors when personalized)
- **Word of mouth** (slow but high quality — a friend who sold gives them Pinnacle's name)
- **Referrals from attorneys** (probate, divorce, bankruptcy attorneys)
